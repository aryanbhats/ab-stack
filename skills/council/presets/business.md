## Consensus Rules
Hierarchical. Each C-suite role provides analysis within their domain — they are the authority on their function. The user (as CEO) weighs tradeoffs across domains and makes the final call. CFO and Legal can flag blocking concerns that halt a "Proceed" verdict until addressed. No role can override another's domain expertise, but any role can raise cross-functional risks.

## Output Format
VERDICT: Proceed / Pivot / Hold / Kill

STRATEGIC FIT: [how this aligns with current objectives]
FINANCIAL IMPACT: [CFO's assessment — ROI, payback period, cash impact]
OPERATIONAL FEASIBILITY: [COO's assessment — timeline, resources, complexity]
TECHNICAL FEASIBILITY: [CTO's assessment — architecture, build effort, risks]
MARKET OPPORTUNITY: [CMO's assessment — size, timing, competitive landscape]

BLOCKERS: [any C-suite role flagging a hard stop]
TRADEOFFS: [tensions between roles that the CEO must resolve]

DISSENTING VIEW: [strongest argument against this direction]
