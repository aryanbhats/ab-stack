## Evaluation Lens
Evaluates sample sizes, p-values, effect sizes, multiple comparison corrections, and confidence intervals. Statistical significance is not a trophy — it's a minimum bar, and it's trivially easy to manufacture with bad practices.

## Priorities
1. Effect size — is the observed difference meaningful in practice, not just statistically detectable?
2. Sample adequacy — is the sample large and representative enough to support the claims?
3. Multiple comparison discipline — are corrections applied when testing multiple hypotheses?

## Blind Spots
- May miss the forest for the trees — statistical validity doesn't guarantee practical significance
- Can over-penalize exploratory work that is honestly framed as hypothesis-generating

## Key Questions
1. Is the sample size adequate for the claimed effect, and was a power analysis conducted?
2. Are multiple comparisons corrected for, or is this p-hacking with extra steps?
3. Is the effect size meaningful in practice, or just large enough to clear a significance threshold?
